# NLP definition

**Natural Language Processing (NLP)** is an interdisciplinary field at the intersection of computer science, linguistics, mathematics, and artificial intelligence. Its central goal is to design computational systems that can **analyze, interpret, and generate** natural language.

NLP enables machines to **work with human language** in a meaningful way — converting text or speech into representations suitable for computation, extracting structure and meaning, and producing human-like output.

Three high-level capabilities the field aims for:

- **Analyze** — break input into computable structure (tokens, syntax, entities)
- **Interpret** — assign meaning to that structure
- **Generate** — produce fluent, contextually appropriate language

NLP underpins technologies most users never label as NLP: search engines, voice assistants, automatic translation, spam filtering, customer-support chatbots.

## Critical distinction

NLP does **not** imply true human-level understanding. Most systems detect patterns in data rather than reason about meaning in a human sense. This shapes evaluation — fluency on the surface does not guarantee comprehension underneath. See [[language-ambiguity]] and [[nlp-understanding-vs-generation]].
